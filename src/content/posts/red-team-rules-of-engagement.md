# Rules Before Tools

A professional red team engagement begins long before the first payload or phishing simulation. It begins with a shared definition of what is authorized, what must be protected, and how the organization will respond if risk increases.

## Define the Boundaries

Rules of engagement should identify in-scope systems, excluded assets, permitted techniques, testing windows, data-handling requirements, emergency contacts, and stop conditions. The team also needs a plan for sensitive discoveries that fall outside the original objective.

## Protect the Organization

Realism does not remove responsibility. Production stability, customer data, safety systems, and third parties require explicit safeguards. Every high-impact action should have a reason, an owner, and a recovery path.

## Preserve the Story

The final value is not a list of tools used. It is the attack path: which control failed, what evidence defenders could have seen, how far the operation progressed, and where detection or containment succeeded.

Clear rules make that story trustworthy. They allow the team to test deeply without confusing effective simulation with unnecessary risk.

**The goal is not to prove that attackers can win. It is to show where defenders can stop them.**
